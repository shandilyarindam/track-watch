# RDSO Guidelines — Track Maintenance Standards

## Temperature Monitoring

Rail temperature must be monitored continuously in sections prone to thermal expansion. As per RDSO Circular No. T/2019/03, any rail temperature exceeding 65°C warrants immediate speed restriction. When ambient temperature crosses 45°C, patrolling frequency shall be increased to every 2 hours.

Track buckling risk increases when rail temperature exceeds the stress-free temperature (SFT) by more than 20°C. Maintenance staff must check fastener tightness, ballast consolidation, and creep marks when temperature exceeds 40°C.

## Deflection and Track Geometry

Track deflection beyond 5mm under standard axle load indicates compromised formation or ballast degradation. As per Indian Railways Way & Works Manual, deflection exceeding 3% of gauge value requires immediate investigation.

Continuous monitoring of track deflection helps identify weak spots before they become safety hazards. Flex sensor readings above 750 analog units (on a 10-bit ADC) correlate with visible track deformation requiring ultrasonic flaw detection (USFD) testing.

## Clearance and Obstruction Detection

Minimum clearance of 25cm must be maintained for under-track drainage and structural integrity inspection. Any foreign object detected within the track clearance zone triggers an automatic alert.

Distance sensor readings below 20cm indicate potential obstruction or structural collapse. Immediate track block must be requested if clearance drops below 15cm as per Safety Circular 2021/S-07.

## Intrusion Detection Protocol

Unauthorized access to railway tracks is governed by the Railways Act, 1989, Section 147. PIR sensor activation near track zones warrants immediate alert to the nearest station master and RPF (Railway Protection Force).

Night-time PIR activations between 22:00 and 05:00 hours require mandatory verification by track patrol staff. Multiple consecutive PIR triggers suggest sustained unauthorized presence and must be escalated.

## Maintenance Response Levels

### Level 1 — NOMINAL
All sensor readings within normal operational parameters. Standard maintenance schedule applies. Next inspection as per scheduled cycle.

### Level 2 — CAUTION
One or more parameters approaching threshold limits. Increase monitoring frequency. Prepare maintenance crew for potential deployment. Document readings for trend analysis.

### Level 3 — CRITICAL
One or more parameters exceeding safe operational limits. Immediate speed restriction to be imposed. Emergency maintenance crew deployment. Station master notification mandatory. Incident report to be filed within 24 hours.
