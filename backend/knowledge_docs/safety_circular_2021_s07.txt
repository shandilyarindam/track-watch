INDIAN RAILWAYS — SAFETY CIRCULAR 2021/S-07
TRACK INSPECTION AND EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURES

1. SCOPE
This circular applies to all zonal railways and production units for track inspection and emergency response during sensor-triggered alerts.

2. TRACK INSPECTION FREQUENCY
2.1 Standard inspection cycle: Once per week for Group A routes, twice monthly for Group B and C routes.
2.2 Enhanced inspection required when automated monitoring systems report CAUTION status for more than 6 consecutive hours.
2.3 Emergency inspection mandatory within 30 minutes of any CRITICAL alert from IoT monitoring systems.

3. SENSOR THRESHOLD PARAMETERS
3.1 Temperature: Normal range 20-40°C. Caution above 40°C. Critical above 55°C.
3.2 Track deflection: Normal below 2%. Caution between 2-5%. Critical above 5%.
3.3 Clearance distance: Normal above 25cm. Caution between 15-25cm. Critical below 15cm.
3.4 Motion intrusion: Any detection during non-maintenance hours triggers immediate verification.

4. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROTOCOL
4.1 On receiving CRITICAL alert:
    a) Impose immediate speed restriction of 30 km/h on affected section.
    b) Notify Section Engineer (SE) and Assistant Divisional Engineer (ADE).
    c) Deploy emergency patrol within 15 minutes.
    d) If track defect confirmed, impose traffic block.
    e) File preliminary report to Divisional Railway Manager (DRM) within 2 hours.

4.2 On receiving CAUTION alert:
    a) Note the alert parameters and timestamp.
    b) Schedule inspection within the next maintenance window.
    c) If multiple CAUTION alerts on same section within 24 hours, treat as CRITICAL.

5. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
5.1 All sensor alerts must be logged in the Section Track Register.
5.2 Maintenance actions taken must reference the triggering alert ID and sensor values.
5.3 Monthly trend reports must be submitted to the Chief Track Engineer.

6. BALLAST AND FORMATION MAINTENANCE
6.1 Ballast contamination exceeding 30% fines requires cleaning or renewal.
6.2 Formation settlement detected via deflection sensors must be investigated using trial pits.
6.3 Drainage adequacy must be verified when distance sensors show reduced clearance.

7. RAIL DEFECT MANAGEMENT
7.1 Any temperature anomaly combined with deflection anomaly requires immediate USFD testing.
7.2 Rail fracture risk increases when temperature differential exceeds 25°C from stress-free temperature.
7.3 Weld joints must be specifically inspected when thermal alerts are triggered.
